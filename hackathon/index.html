The problem

Online surveys underpin polling, social science, and market research. But their anonymity enables fraud—and recent research shows AI bots can now pass attention checks and produce human-like responses at scale. This hackathon tests what actually works to detect and stop bots.

How the hackathon works

Participants are assigned to one of two roles. Apply solo or as a team of 2–3.

Offense: Build bots that attempt to complete a survey.
Defense: Design safeguards to detect or block bots.

Why participate?

  • Applied AI, automation, and survey experience
  • Contribute to research on AI and survey methods
  • List on your résumé as Michigan–Princeton AI Survey Defense Hackathon

Awards and Recognition

1st- and 2nd-place awards will recognize the most cost-effective bots and defenses.

Participants will be acknowledged for their contributions to the research in published papers and any resulting software.

FAQ

Basic programming is enough. Curiosity matters more.

Yes, as a team of 2–3 or individually.

Scripting, automation, AI/LLMs. Rules provided in advance.
